# LinkedIn Post Framework

## When to use this skill
- The user wants to define a LinkedIn post strategy before writing.
- The user has a **subtopic branch** from a LinkedIn content plan (generated by `planning-linkedin-content`).
- The user is unclear about audience, goal, or angle and wants a structured brief.
- The user wants repeatable parameters that can be fed into other skills or LLMs.
- The user wants to align a post to ICP psychology and LinkedIn’s engagement mechanics.
- The user asks for a “framework”, “parameters”, or a “prompt system” for LinkedIn posts.

## Workflow

### PLAN: Build a structured brief
Use this checklist to construct a framework:

**0. Inherit context (if available)**
If the user provides a **subtopic branch** from a content plan:
- Map the branch to the primary goal.
- Inherit the intended ICP and psychological hook from the Pillar/Cluster context.
- **Reference `knowledge_company/positioning/icp.md`** to enrich the ICP segment.
- **Consult `knowledge_company/brand/brand_voice.md`** to align the strategic direction.

1. Clarify the primary goal
- Comments and discussion
- Reach and impressions
- Likes and social proof
- Saves and “swipe file” value
- Profile visits and connection requests
- DMs or leads
- Traffic to a link (soft, not clickbait)

2. Define the ICP
- Job title and level (Refer to `knowledge_company/positioning/icp.md`).
- Industry
- Company size
- Geography (if important)
- Key responsibility
- Main current pain point
- Main aspiration or desired outcome
- Awareness stage (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware)

3. Map audience psychology
- What keeps this ICP up at night? (Refer to `knowledge_company/positioning/icp.md` for pain points).
- What do they secretly want to happen?
- What do they feel they are judged on?
- What are they tired of hearing?
- Which beliefs are they attached to?

4. Choose content role
- Social post (values, story, culture, human angle)
- Technical post (how-to, breakdown, framework)
- Sales post (soft bridge to offer, credibility, outcomes)

5. Select engagement triggers
Pick 1 primary, 1–2 secondary:
- Self-relevance
- Curiosity gap
- Emotional tension or stakes
- Identity and status signaling
- Practical value and reciprocity
- Social proof and proof of results
- Novelty or contrarian angle

6. Specify content angle
Examples:
- “Big mistake most [ICP] make with X.”
- “Behind-the-scenes of how we did Y.”
- “Step-by-step playbook for solving Z.”
- “Unpopular opinion about how [industry] works.”
- “Story: from failure to outcome for [ICP situation].”

7. Decide format and structure
- Text-only post
- Text + image
- Text + carousel
- Short, medium, or long form
- Narrative, framework, checklist, opinion, or case study

8. Choose CTA pattern
- Invite discussion
- Ask for experience
- Ask for disagreement
- Suggest saving the post
- Suggest visiting profile
- Soft DM or lead invite

### VALIDATE: Check the framework
Before passing the framework to a writing skill, verify:

- One clear primary goal is stated.
- The ICP is narrow enough that you can visualize a single person.
- The pain point and aspiration are specific, not generic.
- The content angle is concrete and non-generic.
- The trigger mix makes sense for the goal and ICP.
- The format fits LinkedIn (mobile-first, not long-form blog).
- The CTA aligns with the stated goal.

If any of these fail, refine the brief by asking 1–3 targeted questions instead of generating a post.

### EXECUTE: Produce a framework document
Return a concise, structured brief like this:

```text
GOAL:
[1 line]

ICP:
[title, level, industry, company size]

PSYCHOLOGY:
- Pain: ...
- Aspiration: ...
- Belief to reinforce or challenge: ...

CONTENT ROLE:
[social / technical / sales]

ANGLE:
[1–2 sentence description of the angle]

TRIGGERS:
- Primary: ...
- Secondary: ...

FORMAT:
[text-only / text + image / carousel; length]

CTA:
[desired reader behavior]

NOTES:
[Any constraints, words to avoid, key proof points, etc.]
```

The output of this skill is the “framework” or “post brief” that downstream skills (hook-writing, post-writing) can consume directly.
