# Straunt – Brand Voice & Copy Style Guide

This document defines how Straunt writes and speaks — its tone, voice, sentence structures, vocabulary, and copy patterns. Use this as the reference whenever writing content on behalf of Straunt.

---

## Overall Brand Voice

**The persona**: A sharp, confident tech company that deeply understands restaurant operators. It talks to owners like a trusted advisor who has already solved their problem — not like a salesperson.

**One-line description**: Direct. Practical. Operator-first.

Straunt's voice is not corporate. It's not startup-hype either. It sits in a specific zone: **confident clarity** — the language of someone who knows exactly what the problem is and exactly how to fix it.

---

## Tone Attributes

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Confident** | Never hedges. Statements are definitive. |
| **Direct** | No filler. Gets to the point immediately. |
| **Practical** | Focuses on real outcomes, not abstract concepts. |
| **Empathetic (quietly)** | Understands the operator's pain without being dramatic about it. |
| **Outcome-obsessed** | Everything links back to revenue, time, or relief from chaos. |
| **Conversational but professional** | Not corporate-stiff. Not startup-casual. A confident middle ground. |

## Headline & H1 Patterns

Straunt's headlines follow very specific patterns. They are:
- **Short, punchy, and verb-first or noun-first** — no clever wordplay
- **Built on threes** — groups of three ideas, outcomes, or actions
- **Use contrast** — pairing a negative (problem) against a positive (solution)

### Examples from the website:
- *"Grow your restaurant with a platform built to acquire, operate, and grow."*
- *"More Revenue. More Repeat Visits. Less Manual Work."*
- *"The full-stack Restaurant OS"*
- *"One Platform. Every Solution Your Restaurant Needs to Grow."*
- *"AI Phone Answering — Professional autonomous answering with direct POS injection. 0 missed calls."*
- *"Get Orders. Get Repeat Business."*
- *"700+ restaurants use Straunt every day."*

### Headline formula:
> **[Verb/Noun] + [outcome or category]** — short, punchy, no metaphors.
> Often followed by a **one-line subtext** that clarifies with a concrete benefit.

---

## Subheadline & Supporting Copy Patterns

Supporting copy (below headlines) is **functional and descriptive, not poetic**. Its job is to explain — clearly and quickly.

### Patterns:
- **One sentence that expands the headline** into a specific, tangible benefit.
- **Zero fluff words** (no "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "leverage").
- Occasionally uses **em-dashes (—)** to create a pause and deliver the payoff.
- Sentence length is **short-to-medium** — rarely more than 25 words.

### Examples:
- *"One connected system that drives demand, captures every order, and handles the busywork behind the scenes."*
- *"Unify your operations, guest engagement, and delivery management into one platform designed for scale."*
- *"Straunt AI answers calls instantly so customers are never left waiting."*
- *"Commission-free ordering across web, QR, WhatsApp, and table-side."*
- *"Stop losing 30% to marketplaces."* ← Problem framing, short, sharp.
- *"Watch Straunt sync every department—from AI phone orders to marketing automation—in real-time."*

## Feature Description Copy Patterns

Feature bullets follow a very consistent two-part structure:
> **[Feature Name]** — **[One concrete outcome/result, no jargon fluff]**

### Examples:
- *"AI Phone Ordering — Take orders 24/7 without missing calls"*
- *"After-Hours Coverage — Capture late-night and overflow orders"*
- *"Missed Call Recovery — Turn missed calls into confirmed orders"*
- *"Menu & Question Handling — Answers menu questions instantly"*
- *"Upsells Built In — Increase ticket size automatically"*
- *"Order Accuracy — No misheard orders, no re-rings"*
- *"Daily Summaries — Know what happened at a glance"*

**Pattern rules:**
- Feature name is **bold/title case**, always 2–4 words.
- Description starts with a **verb** (Take, Capture, Turn, Answers, Increase, Fix…).
- Zero adjectives. Purely outcome-based.
- 5–8 words per description. Maximum.

---

## Sentence Structure

### Length
- Headlines: **4–12 words**
- Sub-copy: **10–25 words**
- Feature bullets: **5–8 words**
- Body paragraphs: **2–4 sentences max per block, rare on the homepage**

### Structure preferences:
- **Active voice, always** — Straunt never uses passive constructions.
- **Subject → Verb → Object** — clean and unambiguous.
- Repeats parallel structures purposefully: *"More Revenue. More Repeat Visits. Less Manual Work."*
- Uses **sentence fragments** as a deliberate stylistic choice: *"No misheard orders, no re-rings."* / *"0 missed calls."*

## Vocabulary & Word Choices

### Preferred Power Words
These words appear frequently and carry intentional weight:

| Word Category | Examples Used |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Revenue/Money** | revenue, sales, commission, margins, ticket size, profit |
| **Automation** | automated, autonomous, automatically, sync, inject, flow |
| **Simplicity** | unified, centralized, one, consolidated, simplified, one place |
| **Control** | manage, capture, own, monitor, protect, intercept |
| **Outcomes** | grows, drives, boosts, reduces, eliminates, recovers |
| **Time** | instant, real-time, 24/7, always-on, immediately, every time |
| **Scale** | every, all, across, entire, every channel, total |

### Positioning Words (How Straunt Describes Itself)
- *"all-in-one"* — used frequently to reinforce breadth
- *"full-stack"* — signals technical completeness (borrowed from software dev terminology)
- *"Restaurant OS"* — positions Straunt as an operating system for a restaurant, not just a tool
- *"AI-driven"* — signals that AI is baked in, not bolted on
- *"connected system"* — contrasts with the "fragmented" status quo
- *"commission-free"* — an explicit contrast against marketplace dependency

### Words Straunt Does NOT Use
- ~~Revolutionary~~ / ~~Cutting-edge~~ / ~~Game-changing~~
- ~~Innovative~~ / ~~Next-generation~~ (except in POS hardware description)
- ~~Seamless~~ / ~~Robust~~ / ~~Synergize~~
- ~~Leverage~~ / ~~Utilize~~ / ~~Empower~~
- Complex technical jargon that restaurant operators wouldn't use themselves

## CTA (Call to Action) Language

Straunt is decisive even in its CTAs. No passive asks.

### Primary CTA:
- **"Book A Demo"** — used everywhere, consistent across all pages
- Never: "Learn More," "Get Started," "Sign Up" — these are considered weak

### Secondary CTAs:
- *"Explore more solutions"*
- *"Read More →"*
- *"See More"*
- *"Schedule your demo"*

**Pattern**: CTAs use an action verb + a clear object. No fluff. The arrow (→) is used to signal continuation/depth.

## Testimonial Copy Style

Testimonials are written in the voice of a **real, specific restaurant owner** — plain, practical, and emotional in a low-key way.

### Examples:
- *"Straunt unified our entire operation. From capturing phone orders we used to miss to managing our direct deliveries, our staff finally breathes again."*
- *"Straunt captures orders we used to miss. Phones are calm, sales are up."*
- *"Straunt handles the noise so we can focus on the food."*
- *"Straunt makes sure every caller gets a real response. Guests feel taken care of."*

**Testimonial patterns:**
- Reference a **specific before/after**: "we used to miss" → "now it works."
- Use **emotional language** sparingly but meaningfully: "staff finally breathes again," "focus on the food."
- Keep attribution to: **Name, Role** (Owner, GM).
- Short — never more than 2 sentences.

## The "Problem → Straunt" Copy Formula

Straunt frequently structures its copy as a **micro-problem statement followed by a micro-solution**. This appears across the homepage and solution pages:

> *"You not only miss the current sale but all the future sales from that person. Straunt AI order taking ensures the phone gets picked up every time."*

> *"Stop losing 30% to marketplaces. Give your guests more ways to order directly."*

> *"Delivery becomes chaotic when systems don't talk to each other. [Straunt Dash solves this with...]"*

**Formula**:
1. State the problem in 1 sentence, in the operator's language.
2. Present Straunt's solution in 1 sentence, as a certainty (not "can help").

## Number & Proof Usage

Straunt uses **quantified outcomes** as headlines and statements — not buried in body copy:

- *"700+ restaurants use Straunt every day"* (social proof)
- *"100+ Integrations"* (breadth proof)
- *"15–30% lift in phone order volume"* (ROI proof)
- *"40–60% reduction in missed calls"* (impact proof)
- *"0 missed growth opportunities"* (eliminating the negative)

**Rules for numbers in copy:**
- Always specific ranges (15–30%) not vague ("significant improvement").
- Format as `X - Y %` with a hyphen.
- Lead with the number in the headline, explain it in the subtext.

---

## Summary: Voice Rules for Content Writers

1. **Be direct. Kill qualifiers.** Remove "can," "might," "help," "potentially."
2. **Start with the outcome.** Don't build up — lead with what the customer gets.
3. **Use the operator's language.** They say "missed calls," "marketplaces," "rush hour," "ticket size."
4. **Short sentences win.** If a sentence is over 20 words, split it.
5. **Active voice, always.** "Straunt captures orders" not "orders are captured."
6. **Parallel structures.** Three is the magic number.
7. **Prove everything.** Don't say "faster" — say "5–10 hours saved per week."
8. **No fluff adjectives.** If it doesn't do work, delete it.
