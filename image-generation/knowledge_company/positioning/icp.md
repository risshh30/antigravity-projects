# Straunt – Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

This document defines the primary customer personas Straunt targets. Understanding the ICP helps ensure content, messaging, and marketing efforts reach the right people with the right message.

---

## ICP Overview

Straunt's ideal customer is a **restaurant operator** who is **losing revenue to operational inefficiency and fragmented tools** and is ready to consolidate operations under one platform. They range from single-location independents to small multi-location chains.

---

## ICP 1: The Overwhelmed Independent Restaurant Owner

### Who They Are
- **Business**: Single-location independent restaurant (QSR, casual dining, pizza shop, café, deli)
- **Size**: 5–30 employees
- **Revenue**: $500K–$3M annually
- **Location**: Suburban or urban markets, usually in competitive neighborhoods
- **Tech level**: Uses basic tools (maybe Toast or Square for POS, plus 2–3 delivery apps on separate tablets)

### Their Core Frustrations
- Phone keeps ringing during rush hour and staff can't answer — calls go to voicemail, orders are lost.
- They pay 25–30% commissions to Uber Eats and DoorDash on every order.
- Managing menus across multiple delivery apps is a manual, error-prone nightmare.
- No time to think about marketing — they barely have time to run the restaurant.
- Negative reviews go up before they even know there was a problem.
- Juggling 3–4 different tablets, logins, and dashboards for separate tools.

### What They Want
- One system that handles everything so they can focus on the food.
- Phone calls answered without adding staff.
- Customers coming back without manually setting up promotions.
- To stop paying marketplace commissions.

### Why They Choose Straunt
- The all-in-one pitch resonates immediately — one platform instead of 5 tools.
- AI phone answering removes the biggest single source of lost revenue.
- Straunt Direct gives them a way out of marketplace dependency.

### Decision Trigger
- A period of sustained missed calls and lost orders.
- A frustrating price hike from Uber Eats or DoorDash.
- A competitor who seems to always be busy while they're struggling.

### Job Titles / Decision Makers
- Restaurant Owner
- General Manager (if owner-operator)

## ICP 2: The Growth-Oriented Multi-Location Operator

### Who They Are
- **Business**: 2–10 location restaurant group (QSR chain, pizza chain, café chain, casual dining group)
- **Size**: 30–150 employees across all locations
- **Revenue**: $3M–$15M annually
- **Location**: Regional presence across multiple cities or suburbs
- **Tech level**: Has some infrastructure (a POS system, maybe a loyalty app) but nothing that talks to each other

### Their Core Frustrations
- No unified dashboard — different locations report differently, making oversight difficult.
- Menu changes have to be made manually in multiple places across multiple platforms.
- Marketing is inconsistent across locations — some do email newsletters, some do nothing.
- Labor costs are high and hard to predict — overstaffing or understaffing is common.
- Delivery platform management is chaotic — different tablets at each location.
- Wants to scale but every new location adds operational complexity, not just scale.

### What They Want
- Centralized operational control across all locations.
- Consistent guest experience regardless of which location the customer visits.
- Automated marketing that works at the brand level, not just per location.
- Reporting and insights across the whole business in one view.

### Why They Choose Straunt
- One platform that scales — same system across all locations.
- Centralized menu management means price and item changes push to all locations and all platforms instantly.
- AI operators and marketing automation at scale without proportional headcount increase.
- Virtual brands allow them to generate incremental revenue from existing kitchens.

### Decision Trigger
- Opening a new location and realizing the current system can't scale.
- A full platform audit showing they're paying for 8+ separate tools.
- A new competitor entering their market who operates more efficiently.

### Job Titles / Decision Makers
- Founder / Owner of restaurant group
- VP of Operations
- Director of Marketing (in larger groups)

## ICP 3: The Delivery-Heavy QSR or Fast Casual Owner

### Who They Are
- **Business**: Quick Service Restaurant (burger, pizza, wings, Mexican, sushi delivery-focused)
- **Size**: 3–15 employees
- **Revenue**: $300K–$2M annually
- **Delivery share**: 40–70% of orders come from delivery platforms
- **Tech level**: Relies heavily on Uber Eats and DoorDash; may have a basic POS

### Their Core Frustrations
- Marketplace commissions eating 25–30% of every order — the margins are razor-thin.
- Too dependent on Uber Eats and DoorDash for customer acquisition — if those platforms change algorithms, revenue drops.
- No direct customer relationships — all the customer data belongs to the marketplace.
- Order errors from manual tablet entry into POS.
- No system to run promos or bring customers back directly.

### What They Want
- A commission-free direct ordering channel.
- Ownership of their customer list so they can market to them.
- Automation that handles delivery orders without staff re-entering them.
- The ability to launch a virtual brand from the same kitchen.

### Why They Choose Straunt
- Straunt Direct is exactly the commission-free direct ordering channel they need.
- Straunt Dash eliminates tablet chaos and auto-injects delivery orders into POS.
- Virtual brand support means they can generate more revenue from the same kitchen with zero additional overhead.
- AI marketing means they now have a customer list they can actually reach.

### Decision Trigger
- A commission increase announcement from a major delivery platform.
- Realizing they have zero customer data after 2 years of heavy delivery sales.
- A slow month where delivery platform traffic dropped and they had no backup channel.

### Job Titles / Decision Makers
- Restaurant Owner
- Operations Manager

---

## ICP 4: The Fine Dining / Full-Service Restaurant Manager

### Who They Are
- **Business**: Full-service or fine dining restaurant with table service and reservations
- **Size**: 15–60 employees
- **Revenue**: $1M–$5M annually
- **Key concerns**: Guest experience, reputation, reservation management, repeat clientele

### Their Core Frustrations
- Phone is constantly ringing for reservations, and hosts are pulled away from guests to answer.
- No systematic way to follow up with guests or bring them back.
- Online reputation (Google reviews) is critical but unpredictable.
- Email marketing requires someone to manually build and send campaigns.
- Staff scheduling and payroll are manual and time-consuming.

### What They Want
- Reservation management that doesn't require a human on the phone.
- Automatic follow-up with guests after their visit to drive repeat bookings.
- A system that grows 5-star reviews without asking staff to remind guests.
- Less administrative overhead for scheduling and payroll.

### Why They Choose Straunt
- AI Reservations handles 100% of phone bookings — hosts focus on in-room guests.
- Straunt Reviews proactively intercepts issues and amplifies positive experiences.
- Automated campaigns re-engage previous diners with personalized messages.
- Straunt Ops handles scheduling and payroll in the same system.

### Decision Trigger
- A string of negative reviews that could have been prevented.
- A host overwhelmed by phone volume during peak service hours.
- A realization that their regulars aren't coming back as frequently as expected.

### Job Titles / Decision Makers
- Restaurant Owner / Proprietor
- General Manager
- Front of House Manager

## Psychographic Summary (Across All ICPs)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Mindset** | Action-oriented problem solvers — they want things fixed, not just analyzed |
| **Pain tolerance** | Low — operational chaos is constant; they need relief fast |
| **Technology attitude** | Pragmatic — they'll adopt tech if it visibly saves time and money |
| **Trust signals** | Peer testimonials, ROI metrics, demo/trial before commitment |
| **Decision speed** | Often fast once convinced — pain is acute |
| **Primary motivator** | Revenue recovery + time savings + competitive advantage |

---

## Who Straunt Is NOT For

- Enterprise chains with 100+ locations (may require custom enterprise solutions).
- Ghost kitchens with no front-of-house that have no use for phone AI or reservation systems.
- Restaurants already happy with their current fragmented toolset and not feeling the pain.
- New restaurants with no existing volume to optimize (limited immediate ROI).
