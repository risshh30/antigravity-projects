# AI Citation Patterns

How different AI systems select and cite content. Understanding these patterns is the foundation for Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). Reference this before writing or editing any content intended to appear in AI-generated answers.

---

## How Each AI System Cites Content

### Google AI Overviews

**What gets cited**:
- Clear, direct answers to queries
- Statistics with recent dates
- Step-by-step instructions
- Comparison tables
- Definition blocks
- List-formatted content

**Authority signals it looks for**:
- Domain authority (trusted sites favored)
- E-E-A-T (Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
- Recent publication or update dates
- Visible author credentials
- Citations to other authoritative sources

**Citation count**: Typically 3-8 sources per AI Overview

---

### ChatGPT (with Browsing)

**What gets cited**:
- Specific facts and statistics
- Expert quotes
- Technical explanations
- Recent information (favors freshness)
- Well-structured, scannable content

**Source selection patterns**:
- Favors `.edu`, `.gov`, `.org` domains
- Prioritizes recognized brands and publishers
- Values comprehensive content over thin pages
- Prefers content with clear attribution
- Looks for consensus across multiple sources

**Citation count**: 1-6 sources per response depending on complexity

---

### Perplexity AI

**What gets cited**:
- Recent content (strong freshness bias)
- Authoritative sources
- Clear, quotable standalone statements
- Statistical data with sources
- Primary sources over secondary
- Content matching query intent precisely

**Unique behavior**: Shows "Follow-up Questions" that reveal additional citation opportunities to target.

**Citation count**: Typically 5-10 sources per response — the highest of any AI system

---

### Claude (Knowledge-Based)

**What it values** (helps shape training data visibility):
- Clear, authoritative definitions
- Widely accepted facts and statistics
- Well-established methodologies
- Content from recognized authorities
- Factual accuracy, logical structure, unambiguous language

## Universal Citation Factors (All AI Systems)

These factors apply regardless of which AI engine you are targeting:

| Factor | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Factual accuracy** | Incorrect info won't be cited — ever |
| **Unambiguous language** | AI needs to parse your point clearly |
| **Short paragraphs** | 2-4 sentences max per block |
| **Scannable format** | Headings, lists, tables — not walls of text |
| **Recency** | Published or updated within 12 months |
| **Domain credibility** | Trusted domains get cited more |
| **Source attribution** | Citing others signals your content is credible |
| **Topic focus** | Specific pages on specific topics beat broad pages |

## Optimal Content Structures for Citations

Use these specific patterns in your content to maximize the likelihood of being cited.

### 1. Definition Blocks
AI systems love clear, quotable, standalone definitions.

**Formula**:
```
**[Term]** is [clear category] that [primary function], [key characteristic].
```

**Example**:
> **Search Engine Optimization (SEO)** is a digital marketing practice that improves website visibility in organic search results by optimizing content, technical elements, and authority signals.

---

### 2. Statistic Blocks
Facts with sources are the most citeable content type.

**Formula**:
```
According to [Source], [specific statistic] as of [timeframe].
```

**Example**:
> According to HubSpot's 2024 State of Marketing Report, 82% of marketers actively invest in content marketing, making it the most widely adopted digital marketing strategy.

---

### 3. Q&A Pairs
Question-and-answer formats directly match AI query patterns.

**Formula**:
```markdown
### [Question matching common query]?
[Direct answer in 40-60 words]
[Optional: one sentence of supporting detail]
```

---

### 4. Comparison Tables
Structured comparisons are easy for AI to parse and pull directly.

**Formula**:
```markdown
| Feature | Option A | Option B |
|---------|----------|----------|
| [Factor 1] | [Specific value] | [Specific value] |
| **Best for** | [Use case] | [Use case] |
```

---

### 5. Step-by-Step Processes
Numbered lists for "how to" queries are among the most cited formats.

**Formula**:
```markdown
1. **[Action]** - [Brief explanation]
2. **[Action]** - [Brief explanation]
3. **[Action]** - [Brief explanation]
```

---

### 6. Key Insight Callouts
Visually distinct, quotable statements get pulled into AI answers.

**Formula**:
```markdown
> **Key insight**: [Memorable, authoritative, quotable statement with attribution if possible]
```

---

### 7. Before/After Examples
Concrete transformations demonstrate principles better than explaining them.

**Formula**:
```markdown
**Before**: [Weak example]
**After**: [Strong example]
**Why it's better**: [One-line explanation]
```

## Citation Likelihood Scoring

### High Likelihood ✅
- Content from a recognized authority domain
- Published or updated within 12 months
- Clear, standalone, quotable statements
- Proper source attribution
- Specific statistics with dates
- Structured with headings, lists, tables
- Author credentials visible

### Medium Likelihood ⚠️
- Less-known but quality domain
- Published 1-2 years ago
- Good structure but less scannable
- General industry claims without specific attribution
- No author listed but quality content

### Low Likelihood ❌
- Unknown or low-authority domain
- Published 3+ years ago, not updated
- Vague or ambiguous statements
- No sources cited
- Walls of text with no structure
- Promotional or biased tone
- Factual inconsistencies

---

## Content by Query Type

### Informational ("What is...", "How does...", "Why...")
Structure: Definition → Why it matters → How it works → Examples → Expert quotes/citations

### Comparison ("[A] vs [B]", "Best [category]")
Structure: Quick comparison table → Individual descriptions → Feature-by-feature → "Choose X if..." → Verdict

### How-To ("How to...", "Steps to...")
Structure: Prerequisites → Clear numbered steps → Success indicators → Troubleshooting tips

### Statistical ("How much...", "How many...", "Statistics about...")
Structure: Lead statistic → Source attribution → Context → Related data points → Takeaways

## Optimization Checklist for AI Citations

Before publishing any content targeting AI visibility, verify:

- [ ] At least 3 clear, quotable definitions
- [ ] 5+ specific statistics with sources and dates
- [ ] Q&A format sections covering top queries
- [ ] Comparison table included where relevant
- [ ] Numbered lists used for any processes
- [ ] Published or updated within 12 months
- [ ] Author credentials visible on page
- [ ] External citations to authoritative sources included
- [ ] Clear H2/H3 headings throughout
- [ ] Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences maximum)
- [ ] No promotional or sales-heavy language
- [ ] Technical accuracy verified
