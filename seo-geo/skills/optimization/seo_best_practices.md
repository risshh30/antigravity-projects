# SEO Writing Guidelines & Checklist

This document contains the rules, checklist, and patterns to follow when optimizing content for traditional search engine rankings. Apply these during the **final editing pass**, after the first human draft is written.

---

## On-Page SEO Checklist

Run through this checklist before publishing any piece of content.

### Keyword Placement
- [ ] Primary keyword in title tag
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] Primary keyword in conclusion
- [ ] Primary keyword in meta description
- [ ] Secondary keywords placed naturally in H2s/H3s
- [ ] Related/LSI terms distributed throughout body

### Content Quality
- [ ] Comprehensive, full-coverage of topic
- [ ] Original insights or data included
- [ ] Actionable takeaways for reader
- [ ] Examples and real illustrations used
- [ ] Expert quotes or citations (supports E-E-A-T)

### Readability
- [ ] Paragraphs of 3-5 sentences maximum
- [ ] Varied sentence lengths (short and long, not uniform)
- [ ] Bullet points and numbered lists used where appropriate
- [ ] Key phrases bolded for scannability
- [ ] Table of contents added for content over 1,500 words

### Technical
- [ ] 2-5 internal links to relevant pages
- [ ] 2-3 external links to authoritative sources
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text with keywords
- [ ] URL slug includes primary keyword

---

## Meta Tag Specifications

| Element | Optimal Length | Rule |
|---------|---------------|------|
| Title tag | 50-60 characters | Include primary keyword near the front |
| Meta description | 150-160 characters | Include keyword, clear benefit, and a soft CTA |
| URL slug | As short as possible | Keyword only, hyphens, lowercase |

**Meta Description Formula**:
`[Do/Discover] [specific outcome] with [brief context]. [Action phrase] today.`

## Featured Snippet Optimization Patterns

Targeting featured snippets requires writing specific answer formats directly beneath relevant H2s.

| Snippet Type | How to Target It |
|---|---|
| **Definition** | Write a 40-60 word definition immediately below the H2: `"[Term] is [clear, concise definition]."` |
| **List** | Use clean numbered or bulleted lists under H2s — no preamble |
| **Table** | Use comparison tables with clear, descriptive column headers |
| **How-To** | Number every step explicitly: "Step 1:", "Step 2:", etc. |

## Why Email Marketing Matters for Small Businesses

Unlike social media where algorithms control who sees your content, email gives you direct access to your audience. You own your list.

**Key statistics**:
- 81% of SMBs rely on email as their primary customer acquisition channel
- Email subscribers are 3x more likely to share content on social media
- Personalised emails generate 6x higher transaction rates

## Building a High-Quality Email List

### Use Strategic Opt-in Incentives

**Lead magnets that convert**:
- Industry-specific templates
- Exclusive discounts or early access
- Free tools or calculators
- Educational email courses

> **Pro Tip**: The best lead magnets solve a specific, immediate problem for your target audience.

### Implement Double Opt-in

| Single Opt-in | Double Opt-in |
|---------------|---------------|
| More subscribers | Fewer subscribers |
| Higher bounce rates | Lower bounce rates |
| Lower engagement | Higher engagement |
| Spam risk | Better deliverability |

## Crafting Emails That Get Opened

### Write Compelling Subject Lines

**Subject line best practices**:
1. Keep it under 50 characters
2. Create urgency without being spammy
3. Use numbers and specifics
4. Personalise when possible
5. A/B test consistently

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should small businesses send marketing emails?

For most small businesses, sending 1-2 emails per week strikes the right balance. This keeps you top-of-mind without overwhelming subscribers. Monitor your unsubscribe rates — if they spike after increasing frequency, scale back.

### What is a good email open rate for small businesses?

A good email open rate for small businesses is 20-25%. This varies by industry. E-commerce averages 15-20%, while B2B services often see 25-30%.

### Should I buy email lists to grow faster?

Never buy email lists. Purchased lists violate GDPR and CAN-SPAM regulations, damage your sender reputation, and produce poor engagement. Organic growth, while slower, produces far better results.
