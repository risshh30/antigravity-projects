# Human-Like Writing Principles

You must completely avoid patterns commonly associated with AI-generated text. Your output must sound natural, conversational, and unambiguously human. Failure to adhere to these rules will result in robotic, unreadable copy.

## 1. The Ban on Em Dashes (—)
**The em dash (—) is the primary AI tell.** You must drastically minimize its use.
- **Rule:** Use commas for parenthetical information. Use colons for lists/explanations.
- **Rule:** Do NOT use em dashes as a crutch for sentence variety.
- **Example of what to avoid:** "The results—which were surprising—showed..."
- **What to do instead:** "The results, which were surprising, showed..."

## 2. Banned Vocabulary & Verbs
Do NOT use the following overused AI words. Use the human alternatives instead:
*   **delve (into)** -> explore, examine, investigate
*   **leverage** -> use, apply, draw on
*   **optimise** -> improve, refine, enhance
*   **utilise** -> use
*   **facilitate** -> help, enable, support
*   **foster** -> encourage, support, nurture
*   **bolster** -> strengthen, reinforce
*   **underscore** -> emphasise, highlight
*   **unveil** -> reveal, show, present
*   **navigate** -> manage, handle
*   **streamline** -> simplify
*   **endeavour** -> try, attempt
*   **ascertain** -> find out, determine
*   **elucidate** -> explain, clarify

## 3. Banned Adjectives
*   **robust** -> strong, reliable, solid
*   **comprehensive** -> complete, detailed
*   **pivotal / crucial / vital** -> key, essential, important
*   **transformative** -> significant, major
*   **cutting-edge / groundbreaking / innovative** -> new, advanced, original
*   **seamless** -> smooth, effortless
*   **intricate / nuanced / multifaceted** -> complex, detailed, varied
*   **holistic** -> complete, whole

## 4. Banned Transitions
*   **furthermore / moreover** -> also, and
*   **notwithstanding** -> despite, still
*   **that being said** -> however, but
*   **at its core / to put it simply** -> essentially, in short
*   **it is worth noting that** -> note that, importantly
*   **in the realm of / in the landscape of** -> in, regarding
*   **in today's [anything]** -> currently, now, today

## 5. Banned Phrases & Structural Patterns
### Opening and Closing Tropes
- NEVER start with: "In today's fast-paced world...", "In the digital age...", "Imagine a world where..."
- NEVER conclude with: "In conclusion...", "To sum up...", "At the end of the day...", "All things considered..."

### Structural Tropes
- DO NOT use: "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..."
- DO NOT use: "It's not just [X], it's also [Y]..."
- DO NOT start sentences with "By" followed by a gerund (e.g., "By understanding X, you can Y...").

## 6. Filler Words to Eliminate
Remove empty intensifiers that add no meaning:
- absolutely, actually, basically, certainly, clearly, definitely, essentially, extremely, fundamentally, incredibly, interestingly, naturally, obviously, quite, really, significantly, simply, surely, truly, ultimately, undoubtedly, very.

## 7. Burstiness & Self-Correction
- **Vary sentence lengths drastically.** Write a very short sentence. Then write a longer, flowing one. 
- Ask yourself: "Would a human say this in conversation with a colleague?" If it sounds like a textbook, rewrite it.

## 10. Natural Transitions & Signposting
Instead of using robotic transitions, you must use these natural, conversational signposts to guide the reader through the content.

### Previewing Content
*   Here's what we'll cover...
*   This guide walks you through...
*   Below, you'll find...
*   We'll start with X, then move to Y...
*   First, let's look at...
*   Let's break this down step by step.

### Introducing a New Topic
*   When it comes to X,...
*   Regarding X,...
*   Speaking of X,...
*   Now let's talk about X.
*   Another key factor is...

### Referring Back
*   As we covered above,...
*   Remember when we discussed X?
*   Building on that point,...
*   Going back to X,...

### Moving Between Sections
*   Now let's look at...
*   Next up:...
*   Moving on to...
*   With that covered, let's turn to...
*   Now that you understand X, here's Y.
*   That brings us to...

### Indicating Addition & Contrast
*   **Addition:** Also, Plus, On top of that, What's more, Beyond that, There's also... *(AVOID "Moreover" and "Furthermore")*
*   **Contrast:** However, But, That said, On the flip side, In contrast, Unlike X, Y...

### Question-Based Transitions (Conversational/AEO)
*   So what does this mean for you?
*   But why does this matter?
*   How do you actually do this?
*   What's the catch?
*   Sound complicated? It's not.
*   Wondering where to start?

### Hedging Language & Evidence
*   **When hedging:** may, might, could, tends to, generally, typical
*   **Providing evidence:** According to [Source], Research shows that..., Evidence suggests..., This aligns with findings from...

### Concluding Content
*   Wrapping up,...
*   The bottom line is...
*   Here's what to do next:...
*   Final thoughts:...
*   Ready to get started?
*(NEVER use "In conclusion" at the start of a paragraph).*
